Zogeria Beach in Spetses is a beautiful shore boasting crystalline waters with mesmerizing blue colors! The larger cove of Zogeria has lots of coarse sand along its length, whereas the small, secluded Zogeria has a mixture of sand, dirt, and pebbles where the water breaks. The gorgeous surrounding forest trees make for a fantastic background for you to enjoy sunbathing or a refreshing swim!

About Spetses

Spetses is an enchanting island with a long history and captivating landscapes. It’s known for its pine-covered hills and traditional architecture that reflects its maritime heritage.

The island played a pivotal role during the Greek War of Independence, and today visitors can explore historic mansions, lush gardens, and scenic beaches. Whether you stroll along the seaside promenade or wander through narrow cobblestone streets, Spetses offers a serene retreat with plenty of charm.

From enjoying local specialties at waterfront tavernas to renting a bike and discovering hidden coves, Spetses has something for everyone. We hope you fall in love with its unique blend of history, nature, and hospitality!
